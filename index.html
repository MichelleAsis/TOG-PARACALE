Why Paracale?

Gumaos Beach

Eat. Sleep. Beach. Repeat.
Gumaus Beach is the best choice for a place if you want your mind to be relaxed, not only your mind but also your physical self! You can enjoy either day or night here. Experience the salty water and sand which gives the vibe of being free.

The Church of Our Lady Candelaria

What other site would be better for us to pray than this ancient church?
Interestingly, the town of Paracale was discovered in 1571 by Captain Juan De Salcedo along with the Augustinian Priest Fray Diego de Espinar who introduced the Christianism in the said place. The church of Our Lady of Candelaria was actually was of the first churches to ever built in Camarines Norte.

Maculabo Island

No man is an island, so as you! Relax and and experience the maculabo!
Maculabo is an island of the Calaguas group of islands in Camarines Norte province of the Philippines. Although part of the Calaguas group, the island is under the jurisdiction of the municipality of Paracale, Camarines Norte.

Pulang Daga Beach

Discover the beautiful bond of the sea and the shoreline!
There aren't any rats on this beach, so don't panic. The Bicolano term for "lupa," which meaning ground or earth, gave Pulang Daga its name. One of the best getaways to get away from the busy city is Pulang Daga. A panoramic view of the Pacific Ocean is provided by the stretch of white sand beaches, making it the perfect location for picnickers and swimmers.
